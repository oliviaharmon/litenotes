Explanation

My goal was to create a landing page that is simple and clear in its message, and effective in convincing the viewer to subscribe to the newsletter. I treated the newsletter like a little brand of its own, as something that is useful and desirable to a specific group of people.

I personalised it towards an audience that wants to keep up with the latest in email marketing and use that knowledge to build and maintain a great relationship with their customers.

The author profiles provide extra credibility as the content is written by experts who know their subjects inside out. Being able to attach faces and personalities to the newsletter also makes the experience more genuine and real to the readers. The promise of receiving bonus content creates a sense of exclusivity and makes the readers feel connected to the MailerLite brand.

The visual design is light and transparent. I named the newsletter 'LiteNotes' and created a cover illustration to enrich it with warmth and personality.

I didn't mention all the topics covered in the newsletter (features and updates, business, team and remote culture) because they might not be relevant to all readers, and diverting the content might weaken the brand.

Instead, my idea was to create individual landing pages for interest-based customer segments. Though they would technically lead to the same newsletter, the content would be personalised.
